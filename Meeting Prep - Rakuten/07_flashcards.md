# Flashcards: Rakuten Mobile 5G MiFi Meeting Prep

---

**TERM:** Rakuten WiFi Pocket 5G
**DEFINITION:** The newly announced flagship 5G MiFi device launching March 2026, supporting 5G Sub-6, 4G LTE, and 700 MHz Platinum Band, featuring eSIM, up to 24 simultaneous connections, and theoretical 2.14 Gbps download speed.

---

**TERM:** Rakuten WiFi Pocket Platinum
**DEFINITION:** Current mainstream 4G LTE mobile router. Connects up to 16 devices, 1.4-inch LCD, 2,440 mAh battery (~10 hours continuous use), nanoSIM. OEM: Fuyu Precision Component Co., LTD.

---

**TERM:** Rakuten Turbo 5G
**DEFINITION:** Fixed wireless, plug-and-play home internet CPE router running on Rakuten's 5G network. No installation required.

---

**TERM:** Cisco (Rakuten Partner)
**DEFINITION:** Selected July 2025 to provide its AI-driven Mobile Packet Core Portfolio for Rakuten Mobile's 5G Standalone (SA) network.

---

**TERM:** Nokia (Rakuten Partner)
**DEFINITION:** Selected to provide cloud-native network functions (CNFs) for Rakuten's 5G SA network — authentication, IMS voice, signaling, and user data management.

---

**TERM:** F5 (Rakuten Partner)
**DEFINITION:** Provides security and traffic management CNFs for Rakuten's 5G SA core, including edge firewall and DDoS protection. Partner since Rakuten's 2019 launch.

---

**TERM:** Samsung (Rakuten Partner)
**DEFINITION:** Provider of Open RAN compliant 5G radios (low-band, mid-band, Massive MIMO) for Rakuten's nationwide network.

---

**TERM:** 700 MHz Platinum Band
**DEFINITION:** Critical low-band frequency supported by Rakuten Mobile (and WiFi Pocket 5G) for improved indoor and rural network coverage.

---

**TERM:** Fully Virtualized Cloud-Native Network
**DEFINITION:** Rakuten's disruptive architecture launched in 2019 using Open RAN — first in the world. Separates hardware from software to drastically reduce costs.

---

**TERM:** ¥1 Device Campaign
**DEFINITION:** Rakuten Mobile's aggressive hardware subsidy model: devices (WiFi Pocket 5G, Pocket Platinum) offered for ¥1 with new plan signup.

---

**TERM:** Rakuten SAIKYO Plan
**DEFINITION:** Rakuten's tiered flat-rate pricing: ¥980/mo (up to 3GB) → ¥1,980/mo (up to 20GB) → ¥2,980/mo (unlimited data).

---

**TERM:** Rakuten Mobile Subscriber Count
**DEFINITION:** Approximately 8–10 million subscribers as of 2025.

---

**TERM:** Kazuhiro Suzuki
**DEFINITION:** Co-CEO of Rakuten Mobile.

---

**TERM:** Sharad Sriwastawa
**DEFINITION:** Co-CEO and Chief Technology Officer (CTO) of Rakuten Mobile; also President of Rakuten Symphony.

---

**TERM:** Shunsuke Yazawa
**DEFINITION:** President of Rakuten Mobile.

---

**TERM:** Fuyu Precision Component Co., LTD
**DEFINITION:** OEM manufacturer that supplies the Rakuten WiFi Pocket Platinum mobile router.

---

**TERM:** CIAJ Japan Router Market Forecast
**DEFINITION:** Japanese router market projected to grow from ¥128.9B (FY2025) to ¥134.4B by FY2030, driven by 5G/Wi-Fi 7, enterprise DX, and AI infrastructure.

---

**TERM:** NTT Docomo (Competitor)
**DEFINITION:** Major Japanese MNO competitor in the 5G MiFi market — offers devices like the Fujisoft FS050W.

---

**TERM:** au / KDDI (Competitor)
**DEFINITION:** Japanese MNO competitor offering the "5G Speed Wi-Fi" lineup using hardware by global OEMs (ZTE, Huawei).

---

**TERM:** Rakuten Symphony
**DEFINITION:** Rakuten's global telecom business unit that exports its Symworld telecom OS to international operators. Creates adjacent opportunities for 5G MiFi device vendors to bundle hardware globally.
