# Competitive Intelligence Report: Japan 5G MiFi Market & Rakuten Mobile Strategy

---

## 1. Japan 5G MiFi Market Overview

The Japanese telecom market is experiencing moderate growth, driven by corporate digital transformation (DX) initiatives, generative AI evolution, and continued adoption of 5G Standalone (SA) services. Mobile Wi-Fi routers ("Pocket Wi-Fi") remain highly relevant in Japan for enterprise secure connectivity and inbound tourism. Daily rental rates for Pocket Wi-Fi in 2025 range from $4–$14/day, dropping to ~$3/day for rentals over 30 days.

## 3. Rakuten's Competitive Position

Rakuten Mobile is the youngest and most disruptive MNO in Japan:
- Country's **only cloud-native Open RAN network**
- **Lowest pricing** in the market (Rakuten SAIKYO Plan from ¥980/month)
- Aggressive hardware subsidies (¥1 device campaigns)
- ~8–10 million subscribers as of 2025
- Biggest growth catalyst: **5G SA network rollout (2025–2026)** with Cisco, Nokia, F5, Samsung

---

## 4. Market Trends 2025–2026

### 5G SA and Network Slicing
Carriers transitioning to fully autonomous 5G SA networks. Rakuten partners with Cisco, Nokia, F5 for 5G SA core; Samsung for Open RAN radio.

### 700 MHz Platinum Band
Rakuten's deployment of 700 MHz band (commercialized 2024) dramatically improves indoor and rural coverage — one of the most important trends for portable MiFi adoption.

### Private 5G and Wireless Edge
Enterprises increasingly adopting Private 5G for IoT and mission-critical automation. These require MiFi-class connectivity hardware with dual-mode support.

## 6. Strategic Insights for a 5G MiFi Device Vendor

1. **Align with Open RAN Architecture** – Rakuten uses Samsung (radio) and Rakuten Symphony (cloud platform). Device vendors must ensure hardware integrates with multi-vendor 5G SA networks.

2. **Platinum Band + eSIM are Table Stakes** – New hardware must support **700 MHz** (for indoor coverage) and **eSIM** technology, as evidenced by the Rakuten WiFi Pocket 5G launch.

3. **Cost-Efficient OEM Supply Chain is Critical** – Rakuten heavily subsidizes devices (¥1 campaigns). Vendors must offer high-volume, low-cost manufacturing to maintain operator margins.

4. **Target B2B and Private 5G** – Position high-end 5G routers toward enterprise: Private 5G compatibility, robust security, high simultaneous connection counts (24+) for branch offices and industrial IoT.

5. **Leverage Global Export via Rakuten Symphony** – Rakuten Symphony exports Symworld globally. Partnering in Japan opens doors to bundle compatible MiFi devices with international operators adopting Rakuten's architecture.
